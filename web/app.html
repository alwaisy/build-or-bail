How It Works
Reddit is full of people complaining about real problems. We find those complaints, clean them up, and turn each batch into scored startup ideas. Swipe right to save, left to skip. Grab more batches when you run dry.
Step 01
Pull from Reddit
No account needed. Reddit has a public search API, and we hit it with three different queries at once. Each one targets a different flavor of frustration, so the results don't all look the same.
Query 1: "is there a tool that" / "I'd pay for" ...
Query 2: "I hate having to" / "manually doing" ...
Query 3: "missing feature" / "switched from" ...
→ ~300 raw posts per batch
why do I have to i hate that i have to anyone else dealing with paying too much for wish someone would make i can't believe there's no
Step 02
Cut the junk
Most posts are noise. We only keep ones with real engagement, at least 10 upvotes and 50 comments. Meme subreddits and gaming threads get dropped. If you have already seen a post in a previous batch, we skip it. Every batch is fresh content, no repeats.
Batch 1: 300 posts in → 48 after filter → 12 ideas
Batch 2: 300 posts in → skip 48 seen → 9 ideas
Batch 3: 300 posts in → skip 96 seen → 11 ideas
No post is ever processed twice
Step 03
Let the LLM do the reading
The filtered posts go to an LLM with a strict prompt. We ask it to pull out the core problem, who feels it, what a fix looks like, whether anyone would actually pay, and what already exists. Vague answers get rejected. The prompt forces structure, so every idea comes back in the same format.
Step 04
Score every idea
A number means nothing without context. Each idea gets four scores that add to 100, so you can see exactly where it is strong and where it falls apart.
Market Size / 25
How many people face this. Post frequency, upvotes, cross-subreddit spread. One loud complaint is not a market.
Pain Intensity / 25
Emotional language, urgency, repeat posts from the same person. Chronic pain beats a one-time rant every time.
Solution Gap / 25
"Is there a tool for this" posts. Cobbled-together workarounds in comments. Spreadsheets doing a job that software should do.
Monetization / 25
Professional context, mentions of paying for alternatives, B2B signals. Do not target people who do not pay. They do not pay.
75–100 → Build it
50–74 → Validate first
0–49 → Hard pass
Step 05
Swipe to decide
Build means you want to keep it. Bail means move on. Saved ideas go into your list so you can come back later. Over time, the feed learns what you tend to save, so later batches feel less random.
Step 06
Grab more batches
One batch is 300 posts across three queries. When you run out, hit the button to go deeper. Reddit gives us a cursor for each query, so we paginate into older posts without re-fetching what you already saw. Each new batch is a fresh LLM call, takes 15 to 35 seconds. Already generated batches load instantly from your browser cache.

The Solo Playbook
Find real pain. Pick one idea. Validate it with actual conversations. Then build. No audience, no budget, no excuses.
Stage 1
Find real pain
Time: 3 to 5 hours. Reddit is your main source. Not founder subreddits. Go where the actual sufferers hang out.
"I wish there was"
"why is there no"
"anyone else hate"
"so frustrated with"
"I can't believe there's no tool"
"I've been manually doing"
Search Reddit-wide, not just subreddits. Let the pain find you. Also check Starter Story problem sections, Upwork gigs people pay humans for, and G2/Capterra 1-star and 2-star reviews.
Collect 20 pain statements before moving on. Not 5. Not 10. 20. Exact words people use. Copy-paste them.
Stage 2
Pick one idea
Time: 1 hour. Run each pain through 5 questions. Score 1 point per yes.
Do you personally understand this pain?
Are people already paying to solve it?
Can you build a basic version in under 4 weeks?
Is the customer easy to find online?
Will this still annoy people in 3 years?
---
3+ = worth validating. 2 or less = skip it.
Do not pick the most exciting idea. Pick the one with the highest score where you also understand the customer.
Stage 3
Validate before building
Time: 5 to 10 hours. This is where most people cheat themselves. They skip this, build for 3 months, then find out nobody cares.
The 2-20-200 rule from MicroConf: 2 hours of research, 20 hours of conversations and a basic landing page, 200 hours before you write serious code.
Research checklist: search Reddit for your exact problem. Search Google for existing solutions. Check Indie Hackers and Product Hunt for similar products. Count how many people you can reach to talk to.
The "sell it before you build it" test: write a 3-sentence description. Post it where the sufferers are. Not "I'm building X." Ask: "I keep seeing people struggle with this. Would a tool that does [simple thing] actually be useful to you?"
Do not build anything until at least 5 real people say some version of "yes I would use that."
Stage 4
Talk to real people
Time: 3 to 5 conversations. This is the scariest part for most developers. Do it anyway.
Find people by replying to the Reddit posts where you found the pain. Find them on LinkedIn by job title. Look on Upwork for freelancers who do the manual version of your idea.
1. What is your most painful problem with [area]?
2. How are you solving it today?
3. What happens if you never solve it?
4. If you could wave a magic wand, how would you fix it?
5. Would you pay for that, and roughly how much?
Never mention your idea until question 5. Maybe not even then. You need 15 conversations minimum before you see a pattern. Pattern means 10 out of 15 people describe the same pain in similar words. That is your product.
Checklist
The numbers that matter
Real thresholds. Not guesses.
Pain statements collected: 20 minimum
Idea validation score: 3 out of 5
Reddit posts about the problem: 15+ in 12 months
"Would use it" replies: 5 minimum
Customer conversations: 15 minimum
Same pattern across conversations: 10 out of 15
People who said they'd pay: 3 before building
MVP build time: under 4 weeks
Reality check
What nobody tells you
Landing pages only work if you can drive traffic. Skip them until you have 50 people who already said they want it. Cold outreach gets 5-10% reply rates. Budget 100 messages for 10 conversations. That is math, not failure.
Know who you are building for before you set a price. A US small business might pay $25-100 per month. A Pakistani customer might pay $5 or less. Build in public only works if you have followers. For zero-audience founders, document the journey on Reddit itself. Post in the same communities where you found the pain. That is your audience.
